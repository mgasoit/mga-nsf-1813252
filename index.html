NSF-FUNDED PROJECT

INTRODUCITON

This project is supported by the National Science Foundation under grant number 1813252. In this project, we aim to develop reliable and efficient computational tools for identifying metabolites from tandem mass spectrometry (MS/MS) data. MS/MS is a technology for analyzing biomolecules, e.g., metabolites, which are small compounds typically less than 1500 Da. Improving metabolite identification will advance related areas such as pharmacy, microbiology, and medical science. The lack of fragmentation rules of metabolites in MS/MS instruments is a long-term challenge in metabolite identification. We attempt to address it by using graph grammar and related computational methods. Furthermore, we will incorporate identified fragmentation rules into our new identification tools. Additionally, given the importance of metabolomics, which detects, identifies, and quantifies metabolites in biological samples, we are also working on related educational platforms.
